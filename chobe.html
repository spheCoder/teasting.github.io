Chobe

THE FAMOUS ELEPHANT HERDS OF CHOBE

Chobe National Park

Discover the best time to go and things to do in Chobe National Park, Botswana


Covering almost 11 000 km² (6 836 square miles), Botswana’s Chobe National Park is famous for being a
haven for the biggest elephant populations on the planet. About 120,000 elephants and an unrivalled
multitude of buffalo call the park home.

This must-see wildlife destination includes the Savute Channel and Linyanti River, and offers adventures
such as game drives, private river cruises, excellent birdwatching and exceptional photographic
opportunities. Chobe National Park is also one of the best places in Africa for predator sightings.

For birdwatchers, Chobe has well over 500 species recorded. It’s also a great destination for luxury seekers,
especially in the park’s Savuti region where picture-perfect accommodation lies tucked away from the
crowds.

During dry season, water-dependent animals congregate on the banks of the Chobe River, setting the scene for
some of the best game viewing in Southern Africa. View the park’s wildlife in comfort on a riverboat cruise
and marvel at mighty elephants, elegant plains game and stealthy predators from a unique perspective.
Bring your camera!





Top Experiences In Chobe National Park

Considered one of the best wildlife destinations in Africa, the Chobe National Park is best explored on
morning and afternoon guided game drives.

Recline in comfort on a riverboat cruise as it gently navigates over the still water of the Chobe River and marvel
at the park’s wildlife as they congregate around the river edge.

When To Travel To Chobe National Park

Game viewing in Botswana is best during the dry winter months of May to October, when animals are concentrated at
water sources. The coolest months of June to August are considered the best time of year for a Botswana safari.

For keen birders, the wet summer season from November to March is considered to be the best time for birding, due
to the arrival of migrants.
